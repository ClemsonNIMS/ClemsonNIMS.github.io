Makerday is a celebration of Clemson's creators, innovators, and doers.

Thursday December 6, 3pm @ Watt Atrium

Details

Makerday is an opportunity for any student, club, or organization to show off things that they have created this past year. This is Clemson's biggest show and tell without any limits or scope. Anyone is welcome to bring something they've built, designed, or modified and show it off to the Clemson community!

Makerday saw over 80 project submissions from students, professors, and professionals. Here are a few of the groups we've had in the past.

Screen Printing - Graphic Communications

Clemson's GC department showed students how to creat their own t-shirts using a silk screen printing process. This allowed participants to make professional quality shirts by themselves quickly and easily. Over 150 students made their own Makerday shirt!

Virtual Reality - Immersive Space

The Immersive Space took us to the edges of the universe with a sophisticated simulation. People zipped between planets and across galaxies to visit other worlds. They also demoed several other simulations, games, and drawing programs. Check out the Immersive Space in Watt 308!

Student Made Ice Cream - '55 Exchange

The '55 Exchange is a student run business enterprise that designs, manufactures, sells and serves Clemsonâ€™s world famous ice cream, shakes, and coffee chillers. At Makerday they showed off their brand new cold brew coffee chillers in 3 flavors. Sam Lopane, a masters student doing research on cold brew, served up coffee and knowledge to hundreds of students!

Medical Devices - Nursing

Clemson Nursing students designed dozens of devices that improve patient health and mentality. Projects included a utensil for those with poor motor skills, an oxygen mask with built-in pacifier for infants, an IV tube organizer, 3D printed heart/pacemaker model, and much more!
